Children of Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) carry silent stories of strength, sacrifice, and hope. While their parents work abroad to build better futures, these children face emotional distances, daily challenges, and silent battles at home. This platform sheds light on their voices — their experiences, resilience, and dreams — proving that even across oceans, love and family remain unbreakable.

Who are the Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs)?

Ang Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) ay mga Pilipinong nagtatrabaho at naninirahan sa ibang bansa sa loob ng itinakdang panahon, kadalasan ay ayon sa kontratang kanilang pinasukan. Maraming pamilya, lalo na ang mga magulang, ang pinipiling mangibang-bansa upang matustusan ang pangangailangan ng kanilang mga anak at masiguro ang kanilang kinabukasan, lalo na sa harap ng kahirapan. Sa kasalukuyan, kinikilala ang Pilipinas bilang isa sa mga bansang may pinakamaraming manggagawa sa ibang bansa.

The hard work and courage of OFWs have been recognized countless times, with each of their personal experiences often resonating with others. Every day, they navigate foreign cultures and endure the emotional pain of being separated from their families. However, they are not the only ones facing challenges; their families, especially their children, are also deeply affected by their absence.

Parental Absence Due to Overseas Work

Parents play a crucial role in shaping their children's physical and emotional growth. Physical and mental development begins in childhood—a stage that requires guidance and support from parents. However, in the Philippines, 18% of children live only with their mothers, 4% live only with their fathers, and 7% are raised by their grandparents.

The most common reasons for parental absence include separation, the death of a parent, and parents working abroad. In the Philippines, many parents choose to seek better opportunities by working overseas rather than staying with their families. While this may help address financial problems, it often leaves children behind—those who are most affected and who bear the greatest emotional and social consequences.

Parental absence can have adverse effects on a child’s overall well-being, decision-making, behavior, and coping strategies. Without proper parental guidance, children are more likely to develop high rates of alcoholism, perform poorly in school, and engage in dangerous activities. These consequences often appear as challenges in their academic, social, and emotional well-being.
